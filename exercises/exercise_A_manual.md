# Exercise A — Manual Annotation (~10 min)

For each of the 10 sentences in [`../data/annotation_practice.csv`](../data/annotation_practice.csv), record:

1. **Group mentioned?** (Yes / No)
2. **Which group(s)?** (capture the full span — modifiers and all)
3. **Stance** toward each group — Positive / Negative / Neutral
4. **Policy** directed at the group? — Yes / No

Use the [codebook](../docs/codebook.md) as a reference. After ~10 minutes, **compare with a neighbour** and discuss disagreements.

## Things to watch for

- Sentence 6 — generic "people" without a modifier
- Sentence 7 — group inside a party name
- Sentence 8 — two groups with opposing stances
- Sentence 9 — relational reference ("their families")
- Sentence 10 — intersectional group ("elderly people in rural areas")

There are no fully "correct" answers for every sentence — the point is to surface ambiguity and develop a shared rule for resolving it. That is exactly the work an annotation team does when piloting a codebook.
